Understanding your carbon footprint

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere, driving climate change. Your personal footprint is the total amount of these gases released as a result of your daily actions.

🌡️

What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint measures the total greenhouse gas emissions caused by a person, organisation, or activity — expressed in tonnes or kg of CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e).

India's electricity grid

India's grid emission factor is 0.82 kg CO₂ per kWh — higher than many countries because coal still dominates. As renewables grow, this number drops.

The 1.5°C pathway

To limit warming to 1.5°C, global per-capita emissions must drop to ~2.5 tonnes/year (~6.8 kg/day) by 2030. India's current avg is 4.8 kg/day — close, but rising.

Hidden emissions in shopping

Manufacturing a single smartphone produces ~70 kg CO₂. A T-shirt uses ~2,700 litres of water and emits ~10 kg CO₂. Fast fashion and electronics upgrades add up fast.
